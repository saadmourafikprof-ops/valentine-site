Abstract

This paper introduces Girlfriendium Maximus, a rare emotional-state phenomenon in romantic systems where a single individual; referred to as Marn Marn; simultaneously exhibits maximal levels of cuteness, beauty, and attractiveness. Contrary to classical models that assume trade-offs among these traits, Marn Marn demonstrates non-linear amplification across all three affective dimensions.

We propose a unified emotional field framework where emotional energy transfer is influenced by proximity, sustained interaction, and affection, resulting in persistent mood stabilization and elevated baseline happiness. Observations suggest that this configuration forms a stable emotional singularity rather than a reproducible trait distribution, representing a unique phenomenon within romantic systems.
